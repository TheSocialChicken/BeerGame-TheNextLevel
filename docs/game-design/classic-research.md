**Classic Beer Game Supply Chain Simulation**
=============================================

**Origins**
-----------

The Classic Beer Game is a supply chain simulation that originated at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) in the 1960s, with John D. Sterman publishing his seminal paper on the topic in 1989 [1]. The game has since become a widely used educational tool for teaching supply chain management and dynamic systems thinking.

**The Four Supply Chain Roles**
-----------------------------

In the Classic Beer Game, there are four distinct roles that interact to demonstrate the complexities of a supply chain:

### **Retailer**

*   Receives orders from customers
*   Submits orders to Wholesaler based on customer demand
*   Seeks to minimize holding costs and meet customer demand

### **Wholesaler**

*   Receives orders from Retailer
*   Submits orders to Distributor
*   Manages inventory levels and shipping delays

### **Distributor**

*   Receives orders from Wholesaler
*   Submits orders to Factory
*   Oversees shipping and logistics

### **Factory**

*   Manufactures beer according to production schedule
*   Ships beer to Distributor
*   Manages production and inventory levels

**Game Mechanics**
-----------------

The Classic Beer Game is a simulation of the supply chain, with the following key mechanics:

### **Orders and Shipments**

*   Each player submits orders to their upstream partner (e.g., Retailer to Wholesaler)
*   Orders are fulfilled after a shipping delay
*   Players must manage inventory levels to meet customer demand

### **Inventory and Backlog**

*   Inventory is the quantity of beer held by each player
*   Backlog is the quantity of orders that have been placed but not yet shipped
*   Players incur holding costs for excess inventory and backlog costs for unfilled orders

### **Holding and Backlog Costs**

*   Holding cost: $0.50 per unit per round (penalty for excess inventory)
*   Backlog cost: $1.00 per unit per round (penalty for unfilled orders)

**The Bullwhip Effect**
--------------------

The Bullwhip Effect is a phenomenon in which small changes in customer demand are amplified through the supply chain, leading to large fluctuations in production and inventory levels [2]. The Bullwhip Effect occurs due to:

*   **Demand variability**: Small changes in customer demand can have significant effects on upstream players
*   **Inventory management**: Players may overreact to perceived demand changes, leading to excess inventory or backlog
*   **Order batching**: Players may group orders together, amplifying the effect of demand fluctuations

To measure the Bullwhip Effect, calculate the ratio of variance in factory production to variance in customer demand.

**Standard Game Parameters**
---------------------------

The Classic Beer Game typically uses the following standard parameters:

*   **Rounds**: 26 rounds
*   **Demand pattern**: 4 units/round (weeks 1-4), then 8 units/round (weeks 5-26)
*   **Initial inventory**: 12 units
*   **Shipping delay**: 2 rounds
*   **Order delay**: 2 rounds

**Optimal Play Strategies vs Common Failure Modes**
---------------------------------------------------

### **Optimal Play Strategy**

*   Retailer: Order based on customer demand, with a buffer to account for variability
*   Wholesaler: Manage inventory levels and shipping delays to meet retailer orders
*   Distributor: Ship beer according to production schedule, managing logistics and inventory
*   Factory: Produce beer according to order schedule, minimizing excess inventory

### **Common Failure Modes**

*   **Overproduction**: Excess factory production leads to high holding costs and waste
*   **Stockout**: Insufficient inventory or backlog results in lost sales and customer dissatisfaction
*   **Inventory oscillations**: Fluctuations in inventory levels lead to inefficiencies and waste

**Educational Objectives**
-------------------------

The Classic Beer Game is designed to teach the following concepts:

*   **Supply chain dynamics**: The interplay between demand, production, and inventory management
*   **Feedback loops**: The effects of feedback mechanisms on supply chain behavior
*   **Bullwhip Effect**: The amplification of demand fluctuations through the supply chain

By understanding these concepts, students can develop skills in dynamic systems thinking, supply chain management, and optimization.
